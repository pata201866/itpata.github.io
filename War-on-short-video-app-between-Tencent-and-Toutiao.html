War on short video app between Tencent and Toutiao

June 9, 2018, 11:22 p.m.

China's first startup focused on short video debuted on people's smartphones in the year of 2014.Two days before Tim Cook stood in Flint Center of California,introducing iPhone 6 and iPhone 6 Plus,no one in China would believe that short video industry would involve nearly all of the Chinese Internet giants,such as Tencent,Alibaba,Baidu.The new iPhones were a great success,however,short video industry in China did make much sound until the year of 2017.Tencent,Chinese social media giant,had released its short video app,Weishi,but the app did not stand out.Weishi’s presence in China was dwarfed by that of QQ and Wechat,the super apps that have dominated people's smartphones.Weishi was hardly a success and in 2015 Tencent refused to invest more resources.The short video app was officially shut down in April,2017.

However,in a recent a year,the short video industry became the hottest thing,with users and ventures pouring in,which also attracted not only Chinese Internet giants BAT(Baidu,Alibaba,Tencent),but also fast-growing upstarts,like Toutiao(Bytedance) and Iqiyi.The players gearing up for short video industry marks the beginning of growing competition.

Tencent and Toutiao are at a critical point for user engagement in the year of 2018.The war btween two companies is looming.

On April 11,Tencent's social messaging app Wechat suspended one functionality that allows people to directly watch video shared by their families and friends.The policy could weigh heavily on short video apps which depend heavily on receiving traffic from outside platforms.Douyin,an short video platform created by Toutiao(Bytedance) is one of them.

On May 18,Douyin found its Html5 advertising campaign blocked by Wechat.

On May 22,in an article on its account on WeChat Official Account,Douyin accused Tencent of removing its videos on the video platform owned by Tencent.

On June 1,Tencent sued Toutiao(Bytedance).

On June 2,Bytedance filed a lawsuit against Tencent.

Many people think that Toutiao is not bigger enough to challenge Tencent,although it has been growing strongly for two straight years.
