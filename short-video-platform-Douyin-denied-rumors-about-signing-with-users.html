short video platform Douyin denied rumors about signing with users

2018-06-08T23:10:10+08:00

These days,a word in the circles of short video creators has been that Douyin plans to sign with users with more than 200 thousand followers on its platform.It is said that Douyin wants to tighten its restrictions on users and prevent them from uploading videos to other short video platforms.Today,Douyin denied the rumors.

Yesterday,a screenshot of a post appeared on Weibo.In the screenshot of a post on Wechat Moments,one expressed his or her repulsive attitude towards Douyin,the most popular short video and music video platform in China.He or she wrote that Douyin plans to reach to video uploaders who have over 200 thousand followers and make them sign with Douyin exclusively and offer payments of salaries.He or she worried that if they don't cooperated closely with Douyin,there would be a good chance of being bullied by the platform and not getting much traffic.The wechat user critisized Douyin of "not having principles" with 3 emojis of angry face in the text.

In a statement on Friday on its account on Weibo,a Twitter-like social media network,Douyin denied the rumors.

"There has never been such a policy on Douyin.Creators are an important part of Douyin platform.We have always maintained an open and cooperative attitude of mutual benefits and win-win situations.We have also been maintaining cooperation that is in a variety of forms.Please do not believe the rumors"the short video said in the statement.

Douyin is a short video and music video platform,created by Toutiao,or Bytedance in September 2016.The app has attracted Many young Chinese people and has been growing strongly.China's social media giant,Tencent fears that astonishing growth and scrambled to take out its once fallen short video app,Weishi,and adopted some popular features of Douyin.Many people still have no faith in Weshi,although Tencent has even begun offering major subsidies to video creators on its platform.It is said that Tencent is spending as much as 3 billion yuan(around 460 million dollars) in subsidies to attract uploaders.

The war between Toutiao(Bytedance) and Tencent never ends.Will the aggressive startup beat an Internet giant?Anyway,things can escalate very quickly in terms of Chinese Internet industry.
